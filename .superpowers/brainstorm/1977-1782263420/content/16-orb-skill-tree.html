Orb Skill Tree – Mild PoE-Style Progression

Absorbed abilities (orbs) are placed into a tree. You can use them actively (equip/slot) AND invest them in the tree for passive power growth. Nodes enhance placed orbs.

Core Idea

You absorb "orbs" (the skills/spells from monsters – Single Target Damage, Heal, AoE, Buff/Curse, Traits, etc.).

Each orb can be:

  • Equipped / Slotted for active use in combat (the equipable system we already have).
  • Placed in the Skill Tree for long-term growth and passive bonuses.

Certain nodes in the tree increase the power of any orbs placed in/connected to them.

Simple Tree Concept (Mild Version)

Starting Orb Socket
+10% Orb Damage
+15% Orb Corruption Weight (risk/reward)
Orb Range +1
+1 Stat Scaling on Placed Orbs
Orb CT Cost -1 (but +Limbo effect)
Major Node: Orbs placed here gain +25% power but become "Tainted" (higher corruption)

(Very small example cluster – real tree would have 40-80 nodes, paths that branch)

How It Works with Existing Systems

Orb = Absorbed Ability

  • Comes from monster absorption (Normal/Uncommon/Rare).
  • Can be equipped in your active loadout (the 4-? equip slots we discussed).
  • Can also be placed into the tree (one orb can be both equipped AND invested in the tree).

Tree Investment

  • You allocate orbs into sockets/nodes.
  • Connected nodes apply bonuses to those orbs (damage, range, CT speed, stat scaling, new effects, etc.).
  • Some nodes are risk/reward (more power = more Limbo weight).
  • Certain paths or clusters can be "Saint-aligned" (holy, lower corruption) or "Sinner-aligned" (dark, higher power + higher corruption).

Interaction with Saint vs Sinner

  • Placing an orb on a "Light" node may help purify it or reduce its corruption contribution.
  • Placing on "Dark" nodes increases power but accelerates Limbo manifestations.
  • Dark and Light paths in the tree reinforce the choice: you can't efficiently invest the same orb in both sides.

Why this feels good

  • Every absorbed orb has long-term value and growth (not just "equip or ignore").
  • Mild PoE tree – visual, satisfying to fill, but not overwhelming like full PoE.
  • Directly ties into equipable + corruption system.
  • Supports the Saint/Sinner identity: tree choices literally shape which version of the character you become.
  • Nodes can create build-defining synergies ("orbs placed here now apply a small AoE on hit").

Open questions

  • How many orbs can you have placed in the tree at once? (limited sockets? or unlimited but with diminishing returns?)
  • Do you "spend" the orb when placing it, or is it just assigned (you can move it later)?
  • How do you actually unlock tree nodes? (leveling, absorbing more orbs, reputation, etc.)
