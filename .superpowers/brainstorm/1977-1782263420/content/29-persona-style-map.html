Florence Map – Persona-Style Node Structure

Overview city map with roads and nodes. Entering a node loads a small 3D explorable area (like the current test map).

Structure

Overworld View (City Overview)
• Stylized top-down or angled view of Florence.
• Connected by roads/paths (visual connections between nodes).
• Nodes = distinct parts of the city (e.g. Market District, Cathedral Plaza, Riverside, Slums, etc.).
• Player moves between nodes on the overview (free movement or simple pathing).

Inside a Node (3D Map)
• Loads a small-to-medium 3D area (similar in scale and feel to the current battle_test_map).
• Free movement exploration inside the node.
• Grid/tactical combat only triggers on engagement (Limbo Squares, enemies, events).
• Can contain multiple points of interest, NPCs, hazards, etc.

How Corruption & Systems Fit

World Corruption
• Each node has its own corruption "heat" level.
• Higher corruption in a node = more Limbo Squares, denser shadows, water changes, higher random battle chance.
• Corruption can spread between connected nodes over time if not managed.
• Limbo Squares are placed inside the 3D node maps (invisible until focused).

Exploration Flow
1. On the city overview, choose a node to enter.
2. Load into the 3D map for that district.
3. Freely walk around, talk to NPCs, look for clues/quests.
4. Stepping on a Limbo Square or triggering an encounter loads tactical grid combat (or just teleports you in the 3D space).
5. When done, exit back to the overview map.

Ability Tree / Orbs Interaction
• Focus ability reveals Limbo Squares inside the 3D nodes.
• Tree nodes can affect specific districts (e.g. reduce corruption spread in connected nodes, detect squares city-wide, etc.).
• Saint vs Sinner choices can make certain nodes "safer" or more dangerous over time.

Strengths

  • Feels like Persona’s city navigation (familiar and clean).
  • Keeps 3D maps small and focused (easier to build, load, and populate).
  • Clear separation between "travel" and "explore/combat".
  • Easy to layer corruption visually per node.
  • Supports the hybrid movement system perfectly.

Things to decide

  • How many nodes at launch? (4–8 feels manageable)
  • Do nodes have internal "sub-areas" or are they single load zones?
  • Fast travel between nodes? (risky at high corruption?)
