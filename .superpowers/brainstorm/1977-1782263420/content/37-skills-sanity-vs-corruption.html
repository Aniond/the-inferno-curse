Skills & Orbs: Sanity (Player) vs Corruption (World)

Your question: Should skills primarily affect the player's Sanity or the world's Corruption?

Current Split (locked)
  • Sanity = Player-based personal toll (resource cost, personal strain, risk of "losing control").
  • Corruption = World-based (Limbo Squares, shadows, water color, random battles, NPC behavior, Florence itself).

They are two sides of the same coin, but mechanically and thematically separate.

Option Comparison

Skills Affect Sanity (Player)
Recommended

• When you equip or use an orb, it primarily costs/risks your personal Sanity.
• Stacking duplicates in the tree ramps Sanity cost of that orb line.
• High Sanity cost = higher resource drain, personal debuffs, risk of mental side effects, or "losing control" moments.
• World Corruption is affected indirectly (high personal Sanity can leak and speed up world effects).

Example:
Shadow Claw: High damage, but each use costs Sanity. 3x stacked version costs a lot of Sanity to use but hits very hard.
Church purification reduces the Sanity cost (and overall contribution to world).
Skills Affect Corruption (World)
• Using or equipping orbs directly increases the city's Corruption level.
• More orbs / stronger orbs = faster spread of Limbo Squares, shadows, random battles, NPC paranoia.
• Player Sanity is affected indirectly (living in a more corrupted world drains you).

Example:
Every time you use Shadow Claw, Florence's corruption ticks up. More squares appear in the city. NPCs start whispering about "someone using dark power."

Skills on Sanity (Player) – My Recommendation

  • Fits "Saint vs Sinner" perfectly: Sinners stack power and eat the personal Sanity cost. Saints use Church to keep Sanity clean.
  • Makes the player's internal struggle mechanical and visible in how they play (cost to use abilities).
  • Stacking duplicates becomes a deliberate personal sacrifice ("I want more power, even if it costs my mind").
  • Church purification has a clear role: reduce the Sanity tax on your orbs.
  • World Corruption can still be influenced (high Sanity leaks into the world), but the primary lever stays with player choices and actions.
  • Keeps the two systems distinct so you can design interesting tradeoffs.

Skills on Corruption (World)

  • Very strong thematic link ("my power is literally damning the city").
  • Makes every use of power have city-wide consequences.
  • However, it risks making the world feel punishing for normal play and blurs the player/world split you wanted.

My Clear Opinion

Skills/orbs should primarily affect Sanity (the player), with controlled bleed into world Corruption.

Reasoning:

  • You already established Sanity = player, Corruption = world. Skills are personal power the player chooses to wield, so the direct cost should be personal.
  • This preserves the "Dark and Light cannot coexist" tension and the Saint/Sinner choice at the mechanical level.
  • Stacking in the tree becomes very flavorful: "Each time I slot another Shadow Claw, my Sanity cost goes up, but I hit harder."
  • Church has a strong identity: it helps you manage the personal Sanity cost of your power.
  • World Corruption can still rise from heavy use (via the "leak" from high personal Sanity), but it's not automatic on every cast. This gives you more design levers.

Proposed Exact Rule

  • Equipping an orb contributes to your current Sanity load.
  • Using an orb costs Sanity (base + any stacking from duplicates in the tree).
  • Very high personal Sanity load causes "leak" into world Corruption (faster Limbo Square appearance, more aggressive shadows, etc.).
  • Church purification reduces the Sanity cost of that orb and can lower your overall load.
  • Negative world actions (ignoring people) directly increase world Corruption (as previously established).
A

Primary on Sanity (Recommended)

Skills cost/risk player Sanity. High Sanity leaks into world Corruption. Clean split, strong Saint/Sinner tension.

B

Primary on Corruption

Using skills directly increases world Corruption. Player feels it through environment and personal side effects.

C

Hybrid with Clear Levers

Skills have both a Sanity cost (player) and a Corruption contribution (world), but different orbs and tree nodes can weight them differently.
