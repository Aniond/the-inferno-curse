Limbo Squares — World Corruption Manifestation

Battles in Florence. Limbo Squares appear as the city's corruption grows. Step on one and it randomly teleports you.

Core Concept

These are physical distortions in Florence caused by Limbo bleeding through. They are a direct, player-visible expression of world corruption (separate from your personal Sanity cost).

As world corruption rises (through your actions, absorbed orbs, or story progression), more Limbo Squares appear in the streets and plazas.

How They Work

Appearance:
• Glowing or warped square tiles on the ground (subtle at first, more obvious as corruption increases).
• They pulse faintly or have shifting, unreal shadows.

Effect when stepped on:
• Random teleport to another location in Florence (could be nearby, across the district, or into a temporarily distorted "pocket").
• Small chance of a short combat encounter upon arrival (Limbo Shades or warped enemies).
• Brief disorientation effect (screen shift, sound design of distant voices debating).

Progression:
• Low corruption: Rare, mostly in outlying or "tainted" areas.
• Medium: Appear more frequently in main streets.
• High: Dense in many areas; some squares may chain-teleport or dump you into more dangerous spots.

Integration with Existing Systems

World Corruption
Limbo Squares are primarily driven by the city's corruption level (NPC behavior, environmental changes like water color and shadows, plus your overall actions and absorbed power).

Player Sanity
Stepping on them doesn't directly raise your personal sanity cost, but being repeatedly teleported and ambushed can feel punishing if your sanity is already high (you might have reduced awareness or slower reaction in some designs).

Ability Tree / Orbs
Certain tree nodes could interact with Limbo Squares:
• Detect squares from farther away.
• Reduce chance of teleport or turn the teleport to your advantage.
• Gain a temporary buff after being teleported (risk/reward node).
• Saint-path nodes might even temporarily "stabilize" nearby squares.

Combat & Exploration
Ties directly into the hybrid system: free movement in Florence, but Limbo Squares can force sudden grid-based combat encounters in unexpected places.

Why this fits

  • Perfectly captures Limbo's theme of disorientation and being pulled into uncertainty.
  • Makes world corruption feel tangible and dangerous during normal exploration.
  • Creates emergent moments: you might get teleported near a key location, or into trouble.
  • Scales naturally with the corruption system you described.
  • Gives the ability tree meaningful interaction with the environment.

Things to tune

  • Teleport range and "fairness" (avoid pure frustration).
  • How visible they are before you step on one.
  • Whether some squares are "stable" vs always active.
