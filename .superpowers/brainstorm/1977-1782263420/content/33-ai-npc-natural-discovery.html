Hidden Nodes — AI-Driven NPC Discovery

NPCs are AI-driven. Hidden nodes unlock naturally through rumors, overheard dialogue, and organic conversation — no quest markers or "go talk to this guy" arrows.

How It Works

World State Drives NPC Knowledge
• Each NPC has awareness based on current world corruption, recent events, and player actions.
• As corruption rises in a district, relevant NPCs start talking about "strange creatures," closed areas, weird noises, etc.
• Rumors can spread between NPCs over time (AI conversation simulation or proximity-based knowledge sharing).

Player Discovery
• Player overhears NPCs talking while walking the 3D map.
• Or player initiates conversation with an NPC who has the relevant knowledge.
• Example (as you described):
NPC: "Have you heard that Giardino delle Rose has been closed down due to strange creatures?"
• Walking up and engaging the NPC (or choosing a dialogue option if one appears) unlocks the hidden node on the overview map.

Unlock Effect
• The node appears on the city overview (connected by a road or side path).
• Player can now enter it like any other node.
• Inside the 3D map: free exploration, possible Limbo Squares (if corruption is high), strange creatures (potential new orbs), and story beats.

Integration with Existing Systems

World Corruption
Hidden nodes and their "strange creatures" are direct results of rising world corruption. Resolving or exploring them can lower local corruption (visible on the overview and inside the node).

Player Sanity & Saint vs Sinner
• Investigating a hidden node can raise personal Sanity cost if you use corrupted orbs aggressively.
• Saint path: Church-related rumors may guide you to "redemptive" hidden nodes with purification opportunities.
• Sinner path: Darker rumors lead to higher-risk, higher-reward nodes.

Ability Tree / Orbs
• Hidden nodes are excellent sources of new or rare orbs (especially from the "strange creatures").
• Tree nodes can be unlocked or improved by discovering these areas.
• Focus ability (from Church) helps inside the node once unlocked (reveals Limbo Squares).

Church & Guilds
• Many rumors can originate from or be influenced by Church/Guild NPCs.
• Completing the node’s content can give reputation with the relevant faction.
• Church may have specific hidden nodes tied to their reputation perks.

Exploration Feel
• Feels completely natural because the AI NPCs are reacting to the actual state of the world.
• No artificial "quest log" — you just hear something interesting and decide to check it out.
• Multiple NPCs can spread the same rumor, so you can discover it organically in different places.

Why this feels natural

  • NPCs are AI-driven, so dialogue emerges from world state rather than rigid scripts.
  • Discovery feels like real rumor-spreading in a living city.
  • Player agency: you choose whether to follow up on what you hear.
  • Scales with corruption — more corruption = more rumors and more hidden nodes becoming relevant.
  • Fits the Limbo theme: the city is slowly falling apart and people are talking about it.
