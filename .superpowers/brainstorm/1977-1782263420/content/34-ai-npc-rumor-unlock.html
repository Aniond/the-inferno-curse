Hidden Nodes — AI-Driven Natural Rumor Unlocks

NPCs are AI-driven. Hidden nodes unlock organically through rumors and natural conversation as the world state changes.

Your Rule

"NPC are going to be AI driven so I want it to feel natural"

Unlocking should emerge from the world and NPC behavior, not from quest markers or "go talk to this guy" flags.

How It Works

World State Awareness
• NPCs have knowledge based on current world corruption, recent events, player actions, and what other NPCs have "told" them.
• As corruption rises in a district, relevant NPCs begin talking about strange creatures, closed areas, weird noises, missing people, etc.

Player Discovery
• While exploring a 3D node, the player overhears NPCs talking.
• Example:
NPC A: "Have you heard that Giardino delle Rose has been closed down due to strange creatures?"
• Player walks up and engages the NPC (or chooses a dialogue option if one appears naturally).
• The hidden node unlocks on the city overview and becomes available to enter.

Unlock Effect
• Node appears on the overview map, connected by a road or side path.
• Player can now load into the 3D map for that area (free exploration, Limbo Squares, battles, story beats).
• Resolving the situation inside can lower local world corruption.

Integration with Systems

World Corruption
Hidden nodes and their "strange creatures" are direct results of rising world corruption. More corruption = more rumors spreading and more nodes becoming relevant.

Player Sanity & Saint vs Sinner
• Investigating a hidden node can raise personal Sanity if you use corrupted orbs heavily.
• Saint path: Church-aligned NPCs are more likely to share "redemptive" rumors.
• Sinner path: Darker rumors from certain NPCs lead to higher-risk, higher-reward nodes.

Ability Tree & Orbs
• Hidden nodes are excellent sources of new or rare orbs (especially from the strange creatures).
• Tree nodes can be unlocked or improved by discovering and exploring these areas.

Church & Guilds
• Many rumors originate from or are influenced by Church/Guild NPCs.
• Unlocking and resolving a hidden node can give reputation with the relevant faction.
• Church reputation can make certain rumors appear earlier or more clearly.

Exploration Feel
• Feels completely natural because the AI NPCs are reacting to the actual state of the world.
• No artificial quest log — you just hear something interesting while going about your business and decide to check it out.
• Rumors can spread between NPCs, so you might hear the same story from different people in different nodes.

Why this feels natural with AI-driven NPCs

  • Discovery emerges from the world state and NPC behavior instead of hard triggers.
  • Matches the living, reactive city you want.
  • Player agency: you choose whether to follow up on what you hear.
  • Works beautifully with rising world corruption — more problems = more people talking about them.
  • Supports both Church reputation and organic story beats.

Things to balance

  • How "smart" the rumor spreading needs to be (simple proximity vs deeper AI knowledge graph).
  • Make sure players don't miss key content if they avoid talking to NPCs (perhaps multiple NPCs spread the same rumor).
