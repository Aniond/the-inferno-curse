Orb Skill Tree – No Hard Cap + Duplicate Farming

You can place as many orbs as you want. Farming the same ability multiple times lets you stack power on it — but corruption also stacks.

Your Rule

"No hard cap. If a player wants to farm say shadow claw. Each time its slotted it increases the damage and the corruption cost."

This turns repeated absorption into a deliberate power fantasy with a clear downside.

How Duplicate Farming Works

Example: Shadow Claw (Single Target Damage)

First Shadow Claw slotted in tree:
• Damage: base 8 + STR scaling
• Corruption contribution: +1 hidden weight

Second Shadow Claw slotted (same ability type):
• Damage now: base 8 + STR scaling + bonus from duplicate (e.g. +15-25% on the orb)
• Corruption contribution: +1 +1 = +2 (additive on this orb type)

Third Shadow Claw slotted:
• Damage: base + even higher multiplier from the stack
• Corruption: +3 on this line

The player is consciously choosing to become more powerful and more Limbo-tainted at the same time.

Tree Integration (No Hard Cap)

There is no limit to how many orbs you can place in the tree.

  • You can dump 10 copies of Shadow Claw into the tree if you want.
  • Each duplicate increases the power of the "Shadow Claw" line.
  • Nodes in the tree can further amplify orbs placed in specific clusters (e.g. "Orbs in this cluster gain +10% damage per duplicate of the same type").
  • Corruption scales with every additional copy slotted.

Relation to Equip + Saint/Sinner

Slotting Both

  • An orb can still be equipped for active combat use even while multiple copies are invested in the tree.
  • The tree version powers up the "concept" of that ability.

Saint vs Sinner

  • Sinner path loves this — farm the same strong orb repeatedly for massive power spikes, accepting the corruption explosion.
  • Saint path will probably avoid heavy duplicate farming or will purify the duplicates to keep corruption low (but lose the stacking power).
  • Church/Guilds become even more important as "reset" or "control" tools when you go too deep on duplicates.

Strengths

  • Rewards dedicated farming and grinding specific monsters.
  • Creates very different power curves: "I farmed 7 Shadow Claws" feels earned and dangerous.
  • Perfectly reinforces the hidden corruption system and the Saint/Sinner identity choice.
  • No artificial cap — player agency is high.

Risks / Things to watch

  • Could feel mandatory to farm duplicates for power.
  • Need good ways for Saint players to still feel strong without spamming the same orb.
  • Corruption scaling must stay meaningful so the downside is real.
