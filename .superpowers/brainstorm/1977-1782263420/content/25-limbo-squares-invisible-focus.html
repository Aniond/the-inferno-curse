Limbo Squares — Invisible Until Focused

Completely invisible by default. Only revealed if you have an ability (or tree investment) that lets you "focus."

Your Rule

"Invisible unless you have an ability that allows you to focus."

This makes the hazard feel truly Limbo-like: subtle, deceptive, and only perceptible if you're actively paying attention or attuned.

Two Views of the Same Area

Without Focus Ability
• Streets look completely normal.
• No visual warning.
• You can walk right over Limbo Squares without realizing.
• Teleport feels sudden and unfair — classic Limbo betrayal.
With Focus Ability Active
• Squares become visible (subtle glow, warped texture, faint shadow distortion, or shifting ground).
• You can navigate around them or deliberately use them.
• Creates tension: "Do I risk it or take the long way?"

How Focus Works

Default State
Limbo Squares are 100% invisible during normal exploration. No tell unless you have the right ability equipped or a tree node active.

Focus Ability / Node
• Could be a passive from certain orbs (e.g. a Single Target "detection" or "clarity" variant).
• Or an active toggle (costs a small amount of Sanity or MP to activate for a duration).
• Tree nodes could grant permanent or conditional focus in specific clusters.
• Saint-path nodes might make focus cheaper or always-on in holy areas.
• Sinner-path nodes might let you detect squares but also gain a combat bonus if you deliberately step on one.

Corruption Scaling
Even when focused, higher world corruption makes squares more numerous and their teleports more dangerous (longer range, higher chance of landing in enemies).

Integration with the Rest of the System

World Corruption
Still 100% driven by the city's overall corruption level. Lower corruption = literally fewer invisible traps on the map.

Player Sanity
High personal sanity cost can make focus more expensive (or less reliable), creating a personal penalty on top of the world hazard.

Ability Tree & Orbs
This is a prime place for meaningful investment:
• Early nodes: basic focus range.
• Mid nodes: focus while moving, or mark squares for a short time after focusing.
• Advanced nodes: turn focus into a combat tool (reveal enemy ambushes, or force enemies onto squares).

Saint vs Sinner
• Saint: focus is a tool of clarity and protection. Squares become avoidable or even stabilizable.
• Sinner: focus might reveal squares but also tempt you to use them aggressively for surprise attacks or escapes.

Exploration Feel
Without focus abilities, Florence exploration has a creeping paranoia. With them, it becomes a tactical navigation puzzle layered on top of the corruption progression.

Why this is excellent

  • Makes "focus" a real gameplay verb that feels earned.
  • Perfect Limbo sensation: the world is lying to you until you force yourself to see clearly.
  • Gives the ability tree and specific orbs a strong environmental role.
  • Scales naturally with corruption and player power.
  • Reinforces Saint vs Sinner without forcing it.
