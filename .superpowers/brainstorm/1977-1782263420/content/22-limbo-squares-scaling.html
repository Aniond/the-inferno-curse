Limbo Squares — Scaled by World Corruption

Confirmed: Limbo Squares are driven by the city's overall corruption level. Lower world corruption = fewer squares.

How It Works

The density and danger of Limbo Squares scale directly with Florence's world corruption (separate from your personal Sanity).

World Corruption Limbo Squares Teleport Behavior Combat Risk
Very Low None or extremely rare — —
Low Scattered in tainted districts Short-range, usually safe Very low
Medium Common in many streets Medium range, occasional bad spots Moderate
High Dense, hard to avoid Long range or into dangerous pockets High (frequent ambushes)
Extreme Overwhelming, chains possible Unpredictable, can dump you into mini-limbo zones Very high

Integration with Systems

World Corruption Source
Your equipped orbs, actions, and absorbed souls contribute to Florence's corruption level. The more "Sinner" you go (high personal Sanity + powerful corrupted orbs), the faster squares spread.

Saint vs Sinner
• Saint path (Church-purified holy orbs) actively slows or reverses world corruption spread → fewer squares over time.
• Sinner path accepts faster spread in exchange for stronger orbs and tree stacking.

Ability Tree Nodes
Nodes can help you interact with squares:
• Detect squares earlier
• Reduce teleport distance or danger
• Turn a square into a controlled short blink (Sinner reward)
• Temporarily stabilize an area (Saint reward)

Exploration Feel
As world corruption rises, normal movement in Florence becomes tense. You start routing around known tainted zones or accepting the risk for shortcuts.

Why this works

  • Direct, visible feedback for world corruption without numbers.
  • Makes the Saint vs Sinner choice feel meaningful in the open world.
  • Scales naturally with your progression and choices.
  • Creates dynamic navigation puzzles as the city changes.
