Single Target — Hidden Corruption & World Manifestations

Corruption value is completely hidden. Player only experiences it through the game world changing.

Core Rule (your clarification)

The corruption attached to each equipped ability is an internal, hidden value.

The player never sees a number, percentage, or meter.

Instead, they see the world reacting as the total hidden corruption grows with more equipped abilities and higher-power skills.

Player-Facing Manifestations

Water Color Change
• Fountains and rivers slowly darken or take on a reddish tint
• Water may look oily or have faint floating particles
• Drinking or interacting with water feels "off"
Shadows Appear
• Shadows in corners or under objects lengthen unnaturally
• Shadows sometimes move on their own or detach briefly
• Creates a constant uneasy feeling, especially at night
Random Battles
• Hostile encounters spawn in normally safe areas
• Frequency and aggression increase with higher corruption
• Can feel like the city itself is turning against the player

How it scales with abilities (additive model)

Each ability contributes its hidden base_corruption.

As you equip more (or higher-base ones), every ability’s effective corruption increases additively.

The world manifestations intensify as the total hidden corruption rises.

Example progression (hidden values):

  • Only one low-corruption ability: Subtle water tint, occasional odd shadow
  • 2–3 abilities: Water noticeably darker in multiple locations, shadows occasionally twitch
  • 4+ or high-base skills: Frequent random battles in safe zones, water looks corrupted, persistent moving shadows

Why this works

  • Feels organic and story-driven rather than gamey
  • Matches the unsettling Limbo/Florence tone
  • Corruption has real mechanical teeth (random battles) + atmospheric weight
  • Gives clear feedback without breaking immersion
  • Purification (Church/guilds) can visibly "heal" the world
