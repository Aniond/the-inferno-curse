Single Target — Corruption as Limbo Infection (Dante's Florence)

1200-1300 Italy • Divine Comedy inspired • Florence warped by the first circle of Hell

Setting & Lore

The game is set in real 13th-century Italy, centered on Florence. Limbo — the first circle of Dante’s Inferno — has begun bleeding into the city. It is not fire and brimstone. It is eternal hesitation, melancholy, and the weight of unmade choices.

The player (Guglielmo) absorbs souls/abilities from monsters. Each absorption or certain actions “infects” him with Limbo. Corruption is completely hidden. The player never sees a number.

Limbo Manifestations (what the player actually experiences)

Water & Rivers
• Clear water slowly turns gray, then the dull color of old parchment or stagnant ash.
• Fountains and the Arno feel “heavy” — reflections show faces that aren’t there.
• Later stages: water seems to hesitate before flowing, like it’s deciding whether to move.
Shadows & Light
• Shadows lengthen and linger even when they shouldn’t.
• Sometimes a shadow stays behind after the person or object has moved — a “left behind” soul.
• Torches and sunlight feel dimmer in certain districts, as if the light itself is unsure.
Random Encounters & Atmosphere
• Restless shades (neutral souls) begin appearing in normally safe streets.
• Encounters feel like “interrupted conversations” — enemies hesitate before attacking.
• Occasional distant sighs or the sound of people endlessly debating something just out of sight.

How Limbo Infection Works (tied to abilities & actions)

Every absorbed ability carries a hidden base_corruption (Limbo weight).

When you equip more abilities (or higher-power ones), the effective Limbo infection increases additively:

effective_limbo = base + sum(base of all other equipped abilities)

Certain actions (mercy killings, breaking oaths, delaying decisions in quests) can also add small hidden amounts.

As the total rises, the three manifestation categories above intensify. The world itself starts to feel suspended between life and death — exactly like Limbo.

Design implication for Single Target abilities

Absorbing a martial soul (Strike) feels like choosing violence — it carries Limbo weight because it delays the soul’s proper rest or judgment. Using it in combat pushes Florence (and the player) further into hesitation and melancholy.

Why this fits the setting

  • Limbo is not punishment by pain — it is the pain of never choosing, never moving on.
  • Water, shadows, and random “interrupted” battles perfectly evoke suspended souls.
  • Corruption from absorption/actions directly ties player power to the city’s decay.
  • Purification at the Church or guilds can feel like “forcing a decision” and pushing Limbo back.
