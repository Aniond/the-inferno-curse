Church Purification — The Saint vs The Sinner Choice

Dark and Light cannot coexist. Purification lowers your corruption but weakens the ability. You choose which character you become.

Core Philosophy (your words)

"It lowers your corruption but weakens your corruptions. Dark and Light can not coexist. It makes the player decide which character they want to become. The saint or the sinner."

This is not a minor side choice. It is a defining branch for the entire character.

Visual Comparison

The Sinner (Stay Corrupted)
Shadow Bolt
Damage: High (full base + STR scaling)
Corruption: High (contributes strongly to hidden Limbo)
World Effect: Accelerates shadows, dark water, more random battles
Flavor: Raw power from the absorbed soul. Dangerous, tempting, Limbo-aligned.

You keep the full strength of the darkness.
The Saint (Church Purified)
Holy Strike
Damage: Lower (reduced base or worse scaling)
Corruption: Low / None (removes or heavily reduces your Limbo weight)
World Effect: Slows or reverses manifestations in your presence
Flavor: Redeemed power. Weaker raw output, but gains holy utility and helps push back Limbo.

You choose to become something cleaner, at a real cost.

How the Rule Works

  • Mutual Exclusivity: Once you purify even one ability, you are on the Saint path for that loadout. You cannot freely mix heavy corrupted abilities with holy ones without consequences (or it may be outright blocked).
  • Corruption Reduction: Purifying an ability actively lowers your total hidden Limbo infection. World effects (water color, shadows, random battles) become noticeably milder.
  • Power Tradeoff: The holy version is deliberately weaker in raw damage or speed. The darkness is tempting because it is stronger.
  • Character Identity: This is the moment the player decides: "I am the Saint who redeems power" or "I am the Sinner who wields it anyway."
  • Long-term Impact: Going deep Saint may unlock Church content, unique holy synergies, and slower Limbo spread in Florence. Staying Sinner keeps higher power and possibly unique Limbo-tainted abilities/effects.

Why this is strong design

  • Gives every major ability a real moral/character weight.
  • Creates two very different play experiences and roleplay identities.
  • Makes the hidden corruption system matter — purification is a meaningful sacrifice.
  • "The saint or the sinner" is a perfect thematic hook for Dante's Florence.

Still open

  • Can you have a mixed build at all, or is it strictly one path once you purify the first ability?
  • Does going full Saint give mechanical rewards that offset the power loss (unique abilities, better world state, etc.)?
  • What happens if you later want to "fall" back to the Sinner path?
