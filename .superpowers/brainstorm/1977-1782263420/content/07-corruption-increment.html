Single Target — Equip Stat Interaction + Corruption System

D selected + new rule: each ability carries a corruption value that scales with number of abilities and skill power

D: Equip + Stat Interaction

When you equip a Single Target ability, it can provide a small ongoing stat interaction (bonus or penalty) while equipped. This makes equipping meaningful beyond just "unlocking the button".

Example for "Strike" (Damage):
While equipped: +1 STR (or +1 POW)
Tradeoff: Adds to your personal corruption pressure

Corruption Value per Ability (scales)

Template fields (Damage & Heal)
base_corruption = 1 # the ability's "weight"
corruption_scaling = 0.15 # % increase per extra equipped ability
skill_power = 1.0 # 1.0 = normal, 1.5 = uncommon, 2.0+ = rare/high

# Runtime (calculated when equipped)
effective_corruption = base_corruption * (1 + (corruption_scaling * (total_equipped - 1))) * skill_power

How it increments (your rule)

Every ability you equip adds its own corruption value, but the values themselves get bigger the more abilities you carry and the stronger they are.

Equipped Ability Base Effective (with scaling)
1 Strike (normal) 1 1.0
2 Strike (normal) 1 1.15
2 Ash Hex (uncommon) 3 3.45
3 Strike 1 1.30

Total player corruption = sum of all effective_corruption values (while equipped)

Why this works well

  • Encourages thoughtful equipping (you can't just equip everything)
  • Higher skills / rarer abilities naturally cost more
  • Having "more abilities" itself becomes a tax
  • Perfect hook for Church purification and guild reinterpretation
  • Corruption can affect world perception, NPC reactions, city circles, etc.
