Limbo Squares — Random & Deceptive Teleports

"Just a random square. You think you're safe but it transfers you right back into the enemy."

Confirmed Behavior

Limbo Squares do not look special. They are not glowing runes or obvious traps.

They can appear as any normal floor tile in the Florence exploration area.

Stepping on one triggers a random teleport within the local area.

The twist: the new location often looks safe at first glance… but enemies are right there (or spawn immediately on arrival).

How It Plays

Player Flow Example

1. You clear a small area or think you've found a safe path.
2. You step on what looks like ordinary ground.
3. Teleport to a new spot that appears empty and quiet.
4. Enemies are already present or materialize instantly.
5. You're back in combat, often in a worse position than before.

Corruption Scaling
• Low world corruption: Squares are uncommon. Teleports are usually short and low-risk.
• Medium: More frequent. Good chance of landing near or directly in enemy groups.
• High: Very common. High probability of "safe" landing turning into immediate fight.
• Extreme: Almost every open area has risk. You can get pulled from one fight straight into another.

Design Notes

Purely World Corruption Driven
Density and danger scale only with the city's overall corruption level (not your personal sanity). Lowering world corruption (through Church work, Saint-path play, careful actions) makes these squares rarer and less punishing.

Exploration Tension
During free movement in Florence, you can never fully relax once corruption spreads. "Safe" looking ground becomes a lie.

Combat Integration
This is one of the main ways corruption forces you out of free exploration and into tactical grid combat without warning.

Ability Tree Interaction
Strong nodes here:
• Detect squares before stepping on them.
• Reduce teleport distance or danger.
• Turn the teleport into an intentional short reposition (Sinner reward).
• Gain a temporary advantage after being teleported.
• Temporarily suppress squares in a small area (Saint reward).

Why this fits the theme

  • Perfect Limbo feeling: false hope, sudden reversal, being dragged back into suffering.
  • Makes world corruption feel personal and dangerous.
  • Creates memorable "I thought I was safe…" moments.
  • Scales naturally with the corruption system.

Still to tune

  • How invisible the squares are (completely random vs subtle visual tells).
  • Whether the teleport always tries to put you near enemies or is truly random (with the risk just being statistically high).
