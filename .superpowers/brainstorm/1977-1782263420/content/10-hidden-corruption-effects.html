Single Target — Hidden Corruption & World Manifestation

Corruption is completely hidden. Player experiences it through in-game world changes only.

Your Rule

The corruption value on each ability is internal / hidden. The player never sees a number or bar.

Instead, they see the world reacting as corruption increases with more equipped abilities and higher-power skills.

Manifestation Examples (what the player actually sees)

Low Corruption (1-2 abilities equipped)
• Water in fountains/rivers takes on a slightly darker, murky tint
• Shadows in corners seem a little longer than they should
• Occasional distant, unexplained sounds or movement
Medium Corruption (3-4 abilities / some higher skills)
• Water starts shifting toward reddish or oily black in certain areas
• Shadows detach and move independently for brief moments
• Random hostile encounters begin appearing in "safe" zones (tavern streets, etc.)
High Corruption (many / strong abilities)
• Water visibly darkens and may "pulse" or have floating ash-like particles
• Persistent moving shadows that follow the player
• Frequent, aggressive random battles even in normally peaceful locations

How this ties to the Single Target template

Updated Fields (hidden corruption)
base_corruption = 1 # hidden internal value
# effective_corruption is calculated the same additive way
# but ONLY feeds world systems — never shown to player

equip_stat_mod = { "STR": +1 }

# These drive the effects above when total effective corruption rises

Important design notes

  • No corruption meter, percentage, or number is ever displayed in UI
  • Effects are environmental, atmospheric, and mechanical (random battles)
  • Stronger abilities (higher base_corruption) accelerate the visible changes faster
  • Equipping more abilities directly makes the world "worse" around the player
  • This creates the "price of power" without breaking immersion
  • Purification (Church/guilds) would visibly clean up these effects

Why this fits the game

  • Matches Limbo/Florence unsettling, memory-delayed tone
  • Corruption feels like a living, changing world rather than a stat
  • Gives clear feedback without numbers
  • Random battles become a meaningful downside
