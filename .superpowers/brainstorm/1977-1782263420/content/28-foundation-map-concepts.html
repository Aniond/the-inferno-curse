Foundations Check + Map Concepts

Pausing on Church details for now. Confirming current foundations and starting to sketch city/world map needs.

Current Foundations (what we have so far)
  • Absorbed Orbs: Skills/spells from monsters. Equipable. Can be placed in the ability tree (no hard cap).
  • Ability Tree: Mild PoE-style. Place orbs in nodes. Nodes boost power of placed orbs. Duplicates of the same orb increase power but also increase Sanity cost.
  • Sanity (Player): Personal cost. Ramps with stacking duplicates. Affects ability use cost and personal feel.
  • Corruption (World): Affects Florence, NPCs, environment. Drives Limbo Squares, water color, shadows, random battles.
  • Limbo Squares: Invisible random floor hazards. Scale with world corruption. Step on one = random teleport (often drops you into enemies). Invisible unless you have Focus ability.
  • Saint vs Sinner: Dark and Light cannot coexist. Big character choice.
  • Church: Purifies corrupted orbs into holy versions (lowers corruption, weakens power). Reputation via quests unlocks perks (including Focus ability at higher levels).
  • Exploration: Free movement in Florence until engagement. Grid/tactical combat only when triggered.
  • Single Target: Starting ability type (Damage + Heal variants) with clear template structure.

Map Foundation Concepts

The maps need to support:

  • Free exploration that feels dangerous as world corruption rises (Limbo Squares, random battles).
  • Clear progression of corruption effects across areas.
  • Church and Guild locations as meaningful hubs.
  • Ability tree / orb investment mattering in the world (e.g., focus for squares, holy orbs affecting corruption spread).
Florence (City Map)
• Multiple districts with distinct identities.
• Corruption "heat map" overlay (visual only — not a number the player sees).
• Limbo Squares appear more densely in high-corruption zones.
• Church as central hub (unlocks at certain point, reputation gated perks).
• Guild halls scattered (different reinterpretation options).
• Safe-ish zones that slowly corrupt over time if you don't manage world corruption.
Overworld / World Map
• Florence as main hub.
• Connected regions (countryside, other towns, wild areas).
• Some regions start "clean" and can become tainted if you ignore corruption.
• Fast travel points that might get blocked or risky at high corruption.
• Monster types tied to regions (different orbs available).
• Story/narrative beats that spread Limbo if not addressed.

Benefits of this direction

  • World feels alive and reactive to player choices (Saint vs Sinner has real map consequences).
  • Exploration stays tense even in "safe" areas once corruption spreads.
  • Church and Guilds become natural hubs on the map.
  • Limbo Squares and other hazards give exploration meaning.

  • Do corrupted areas ever "heal" on their own if you lower world corruption?
